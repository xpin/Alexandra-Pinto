Research Interests

I have special interest in Artificial Intelligence and in pushing forward the boundaries of information transmission and computation. I enjoy doing so by combining interdisciplinary research, team work and entrepreneurship. I have found that my maximum achievements are in positions where I am a bridge between Software Engineering Management and Business-Customer negotiations. I like modeling nonlinear physical phenomena in hardware and software networks and tackling data science problems for different market sectors. I have a solid background in Bayesian networks, Statistics and Theoretical Neurosciences for Machine Learning applications.

During my master studies at Zurich, I specialised in coursework for research on Neural Systems and Computation, with emphasis in Neural Computation and Information theory, in addition to Neurotechnologies, Neuromorphic Engineering and Quantum Information Processing. I would like to explain the neural information transmission and processes with a solid theoretical foundation. I am particularly interested in long range correlations as can be observed in EEG signals given that these signals are measured simultaneously in different parts of the skull but they correspond to the same functional task. I found a relationship between a proposed synaptic model and what could be seen as a possible mesoscopic scale phase transition prediction by considering long range correlations through the Bose Einstein condensate with interesting intersection with Quantum Phenomena.
