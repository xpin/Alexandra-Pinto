Research Interests

I have a special interest in Artificial Intelligence, information transmission, and computation. I enjoy doing so by combining interdisciplinary research between Software Engineering, Management, and Business-Customer negotiations. I like modeling nonlinear physical phenomena in hardware and software networks and tackling data science problems for different market sectors. I have a solid background in Bayesian networks, Statistics, and Theoretical Neurosciences for Machine Learning applications.

During my master studies at Zurich, I specialized in coursework for research on Neural Systems and Computation, with an emphasis on Neural Computation and Information theory, in addition, the Neurotechnologies, Neuromorphic Engineering, and Quantum Information Processing. I am particularly interested in long-range correlations as can be observed in EEG signals and in modeling synaptic signaling for dynamic phase transitions.
