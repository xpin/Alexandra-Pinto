  • I had experience in experimental physics, exploring naturally occurring noise in VO2, which was a very delicate signal that needed careful isolation of noise using braided gold cables, this signal explained the coupling of electrons in this material as it was immersed in liquid nitrogen, the signal acquisition was made with an spectrum analyzer and Labview, the data was later processed using Matlab. As my senior research project, I studied the effects of noise in an artificial neuron, studying how different kinds of noise caused amplification of an input signal, data was automatically taken using Labview to control the input to the system and to take data, which was later processed with Matlab. I also studied the effect of X-rays on DNA damage, I developed a 3D model of DNA in Matlab based on PDB data, where each atom is identified, located in space and ion radius is assigned. The program can create a straight DNA strand of any length given a string of nitrogen bases. Strands as long as 10.000 nitrogen bases with a total of 6'370.000 atoms were created to estimate the damage that X-rays cause. To simulate X-rays realistically the program GEANT4 was used to get the spatial distribution of HO- and H+ after X-rays go through water.
